Grounding practices

Use these anytime you need to return to the present moment. Even two or three minutes can create a meaningful shift.

Panic script — when things feel like too much

1. Name 1 safe place right now (even just the floor beneath your feet).
2. Notice 1 color in the room.
3. Press your feet into the floor and whisper: "I am here. I am okay right now."
4. Exhale slowly and completely.

5-4-3-2-1 body awareness
Name out loud (or write):
  • 5 things you can see
  • 4 things you can physically feel
  • 3 things you can hear
  • 2 things you can smell
  • 1 thing you can taste
Weighted blanket hug
Wrap your arms around yourself and squeeze for about 20 seconds. Deep pressure touch releases oxytocin, the calming hormone.
Hand over heart
Place one or both hands over your heart. Feel your own warmth. Breathe slowly. You can silently say something kind to yourself — whatever comes. There's no script required.
Hot cocoa breath
Inhale deeply through your nose, imagining the smell of something warm and comforting. Exhale slowly through slightly pursed lips — like blowing on something hot to cool it. Repeat 3–5 times.
Tactile awareness
Slowly describe 3 textures you can touch right now. Say the words out loud if you can.

No single technique works for everyone every time. Try a few and notice which one lands best for you.
